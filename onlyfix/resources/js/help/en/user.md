# Help – User Guide

Welcome to OnlyFix! This guide will help you navigate the application so you can manage your car repair tickets quickly and efficiently.

---

## Dashboard

The dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It gives you a quick overview of your account:

- **Cars** – number of vehicles you have registered
- **Total tickets** – all repair requests you have submitted
- **Open tickets** – repairs currently in progress
- **Closed tickets** – successfully completed repairs

Your most recent tickets and cars are also shown so you can reach them in one click.

---

## Managing Cars

### Adding a new car

1. Click **My Cars** in the sidebar.
2. Click the **Add Car** button.
3. Fill in the details: license plate, color, VIN (chassis number).
4. Click **Save**.

### Editing a car

1. Open the car list.
2. Click on the car you want to update.
3. Choose **Edit**.
4. Make your changes and save.

### Deleting a car

Make sure there are no active tickets assigned to the car before deleting it.

---

## Tickets

Tickets are your repair requests. Each ticket is linked to one of your cars and can contain one or more problems.

### Creating a new ticket

1. Click **My Tickets** in the sidebar.
2. Click **New Ticket**.
3. Select which of your cars needs servicing.
4. Check the problems from the list (e.g. engine, brakes, suspension).
5. Set the priority: low, medium, high, or critical.
6. Click **Submit Ticket**.

### Ticket statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Ticket submitted, waiting for a mechanic |
| **Assigned** | A mechanic has accepted the task |
| **In Progress** | Repair is actively being worked on |
| **Completed** | Repair has been finished |
| **Closed** | Ticket has been closed |

### Closing a ticket

If you are satisfied with the repair, you can close the ticket yourself from the detail view using the **Close Ticket** button.

## Profile and Settings

- **Edit profile**: Change your name and email in Settings / Profile.
- **Change password**: Settings / Password.
- **Appearance**: Choose between light, dark, or system theme (Settings / Appearance).
- **Two-factor authentication**: Increase account security (Settings / Two-Factor Authentication).

---

## Tips and FAQ

**Why can't I edit my ticket?**
Only open tickets can be modified. Once a mechanic has accepted the ticket, editing is restricted.

**How do I track the progress of my repair?**
On the ticket detail view, a timeline shows all status changes and timestamps.

**Can I delete a car that has tickets attached?**
No. Close or delete all related tickets first.

**I forgot my password – what do I do?**
On the login page, click the **Forgot Password** link and we will send a reset link to your email.
